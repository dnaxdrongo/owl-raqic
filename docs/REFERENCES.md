# Scholarly references used in the code and README

The citations below support specific mathematical or algorithmic precedents. They do not
establish that simulated observer windows are conscious, biological systems use p-adic
representations, or RAQIC provides quantum advantage. The complete reference metadata and
claim limits are available in `REFERENCES.json`.

- **R01:** J. Riddle and J. W. Schooler, “Hierarchical consciousness: the Nested Observer
  Windows model,” *Neuroscience of Consciousness*, vol. 2024, no. 1, niae010, 2024.
  [doi:10.1093/nc/niae010](https://doi.org/10.1093/nc/niae010).
- **R13:** F. Q. Gouvêa, *p-adic Numbers: An Introduction*. Springer, 1997.
- **R20:** M. A. Nielsen and I. L. Chuang, *Quantum Computation and Quantum Information*,
  10th anniversary ed. Cambridge University Press, 2010.
- **R23:** V. Bergholm, J. J. Vartiainen, M. Möttönen, and M. M. Salomaa, “Quantum circuits
  with uniformly controlled one-qubit gates,” *Physical Review A*, vol. 71, 052330, 2005.
  [doi:10.1103/PhysRevA.71.052330](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevA.71.052330).
- **R24:** N. Jiang and L. Li, “Doubly robust off-policy value evaluation for reinforcement
  learning,” *Proceedings of Machine Learning Research*, vol. 48, pp. 652–661, 2016.
- **R29:** C. Burges et al., “Learning to rank using gradient descent,” *Proceedings of the
  22nd ICML*, pp. 89–96, 2005.
  [doi:10.1145/1102351.1102363](https://doi.org/10.1145/1102351.1102363).
- **R30:** Z. Cao et al., “Learning to rank: From pairwise approach to listwise approach,”

- **R38:** B. L. Nelson and F. J. Matejcik, “Using common random numbers for indifference-zone
